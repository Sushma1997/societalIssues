What is Child Labour?

Child labour refers to the employment of children in any work that deprives children of their childhood, interferes with their ability to attend regular school, and that is mentally, physically, socially or morally dangerous and harmful.In its most extreme forms, child labour involves children being enslaved, separated from their families, exposed to serious hazards and illnesses and/or left to fend for themselves on the streets of large cities â€“ often at a very early age. This practice is considered exploitative by many international organisations.

2) Child labor perpetuates poverty, unemployment, illiteracy, population growth, and other social problems.

3) If we can't begin to agree on fundamentals, such as the elimination of the most abusive forms of child labor, then we really are not ready to march forward into the future.
